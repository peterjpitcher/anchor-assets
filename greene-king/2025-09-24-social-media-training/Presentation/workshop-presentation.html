Quick Audit: Start Here

Score yourself 0–5

  • Google Business Profile: hours, menu, phone, photos up to date?
  • Last 7 days: at least 3 Facebook and 3 Instagram posts?
  • Last 5 posts: each has a clear call to action?
  • Photos: bright, in‑focus, people where possible?
  • Events: listed on Facebook and mirrored on Google Business Profile?

Pick two gaps to fix in the next 7 days.

4-Step Post Check

  1. Goal + Call to Action: One outcome per post (book, call, directions, RSVP). State the exact ask.
  2. Shot: Bright, single subject that tells the story. People over plates; daylight over flash.
  3. Caption Hook: Lead with the strongest detail, then the ask. Keep it tight and clear.
  4. Timing: Post when guests decide (lunch, after work, weekend mornings). Add reminders.

Compliance tone: Celebrate experience and community. Avoid glorifying excessive drinking. Always include a clear call to action aligned to the guest journey.

Channel Maturity: 3 Simple Stages

1. Start

  • Post natively on Facebook and Instagram.
  • Use your phone to take clear, daylight photos.
  • Avoid screenshot images.

2. Build

  • Use social scheduling tools to plan a week ahead.
  • Create professional‑looking assets in Canva.

3. Expand

  • Create customised content on your website.
  • Add dedicated calls to action on those pages.
  • Drive guests to one place to convert.

Rule of thumb: Don’t expand until basics are consistent for 4+ weeks.

Facebook: Do / Don’t / Focus

Do

  • Create Facebook Events for gigs, sport, quizzes; share updates.
  • Post specials and local moments with clear CTAs.
  • Use Events + Posts + pinned offers to drive action.

Don’t

  • Post walls of flyers or long text with no hook.
  • Share endless third‑party links or off‑topic memes.

Setup Musts

  • Correct hours; “Call Now” button; menu/booking link.
  • Messaging enabled; cover photo up to date.
  • Use approved brand lockup on images where it helps recognition.

Goal

Drive bookings, RSVPs, calls. Use a strong hook and a single ask.

Instagram: Do / Don’t / Focus

Do

  • Show atmosphere and people; Reels for vibe; Stories daily.
  • Add location tag; include a call to action in captions or story buttons.

Don’t

  • Overuse filters; dark food shots; text‑heavy tiles.
  • Post without a next step in mind.

Setup Musts

  • Bio with a clear call to action and a link tool; highlights for menu/events.
  • Consistent location tagging and brand‑safe visuals.
  • Use approved brand lockup on images where it helps recognition.

Focus

Create desire that feeds bookings and Facebook actions. Think “scroll‑stopping” image then a clear call to action.

Google Business Profile: Do / Don’t / Focus

Do

  • Keep hours, menu, phone accurate; add weekly photos.
  • Post weekly Updates for events/offers; reply to reviews fast.

Don’t

  • Leave outdated hours or old menus.
  • Ignore reviews or upload low‑res photos.

Setup Musts

  • Booking and call buttons; correct categories and attributes.
  • Holiday hours and photo cover updated seasonally.

Focus

Capture intent at decision moment: directions, calls, “book” clicks.

7‑Day Action Plan (Simple)

  1. Day 1: Update Google Business Profile info and add 3 fresh photos.
  2. Day 2: Shoot 6 bright photos in daylight (people, hero dish, exterior).
  3. Day 3: Make 4 image posts in Canva using the 4‑Step Post Check.
  4. Day 4: Schedule 3 Facebook and 3 Instagram posts in Meta Business Suite.
  5. Day 5: Create one Facebook Event for next week; share to Instagram Stories.
  6. Day 6: Reply to reviews and top comments; post one reminder.
  7. Day 7: Review what worked; queue next week’s posts.

De‑prioritise for now: X/Twitter (needs high frequency, low local ROI); TikTok (high creativity/time; optional if you have resource and a clear concept).

Appendix: Helpful Tools

Reference only — not presented live. Share with attendees after the session.

Design

  • Canva — Free; Pro ~£100/year. Fast templates for posts; easy brand lockup overlays.
  • remove.bg — Quick background removal (free tier).
  • Photopea — Free Photoshop‑like editor in the browser.

Scheduling

  • Meta Business Suite — Free. Schedule Facebook and Instagram; basic analytics.
  • Buffer — From ~£6–£12/user/month. Simple, reliable scheduling; link in bio tools.
  • Later — From ~£16/month. Strong Instagram features, media library, link in bio.
  • Hootsuite — Team scheduling, approvals, reporting (higher cost).

Recommended Posting Times

  • Facebook: Weekdays 11:30–13:30 and 16:30–18:30; weekend late morning 10:30–12:00. Events: create 7–14 days before; reminders 24h and 3h before.
  • Instagram: Weekdays 12:00–14:00 and 18:00–20:00; Stories earlier in the day; Reels Thu–Sun late afternoon.
  • Google Business Profile: Updates Mon/Tue morning; photo uploads Thu/Fri morning; review replies within 24–48 hours.

Use your page insights: if your crowd skews after-work or weekend sport, bias posts to those windows.

Image & Video Specs

  • Facebook image posts: 1200×1200 or 1080×1350 (portrait) for feed.
  • Instagram grid: 1080×1080 (square) or 1080×1350 (portrait, best).
  • Stories/Reels: 1080×1920 (9:16). Keep key text within safe margins.
  • Compression: export JPG at 80–85% quality; video 1080p vertical.

Phone Photography Tips

  • Shoot near windows; turn off heat lamps; avoid mixed lighting.
  • Tap to focus, lower exposure slightly for richer colour.
  • Clean lens; shoot multiple angles; include people hands/faces.
  • Background: remove clutter; simple surfaces; add one prop (cutlery, napkin).

Caption Formulas

  • Hook + Specific + Call to Action: “Live jazz Fri 8pm — book now → [link]”.
  • Offer + Proof + Call to Action: “Sunday roasts sold out by 2pm — pre‑order for next Sun → [link]”.
  • Event + Detail + Call to Action: “Quiz Night Wed 7:30 — teams of 4 — RSVP → [FB Event]”.

Content Ideas

  • Events: quizzes, live music, sport fixtures, tastings.
  • Food & Drink: hero dishes, new pours, behind‑the‑scenes prep.
  • People & Place: team intros, regulars (with consent), cozy corners.
  • Community: charity nights, local partnerships, dog of the week.

Reviews: Fast Response Template

  • Thank + Acknowledge: “Thanks for taking the time to review us…”
  • Address specifics: “Sorry the roast was delayed; we were short on yorkies at 1pm.”
  • Make it right: “Please email manager@[yourpub].co.uk so we can invite you back.”
  • Sign off human: name + role.

Compliance & Safety (Hospitality)

  • Avoid glorifying excessive drinking; show experience, food, community.
  • Age‑gating: don’t depict or target under‑18s; be careful with shots.
  • Music/photos: use your own images or licensed/royalty‑free assets.

Accessibility

  • Add alt text where possible; describe the key visual.
  • Use high‑contrast text; avoid text‑heavy images.
  • Captions/subtitles for videos; consider quiet/no‑audio viewing.

Links, Tracking, and CTAs

  • Use a single “book now/menu” link (or buttons) per post.
  • Add UTM tags to booking links to measure from socials.
  • Link in bio tools: Buffer/Linktree/Later to route to your pages.

Website Landing Tips

  • One page per promo/event with a bold call to action (book/call).
  • Clear hours, phone, map, parking; quick‑load images.
  • Reuse page links in social posts and Google Business Profile Updates.

Permissions & Competitions

  • Get verbal consent for guest photos; written if featuring prominently.
  • Competitions: state prize, eligibility, closing date, how to enter, how winners are chosen; avoid “share to win” requirements.

Benefit: Scheduling in weekly batches reduces time pressure and keeps cadence consistent. Start with Meta (free), upgrade if you outgrow it.
